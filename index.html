1. Raw: Feel the palm of your hand, just below your thumb. It’s a little soft and fleshy.This is what raw steak should feel like when you touch it.
2. Rare: Now bring your thumb to your pointer finger, and touch that same part of yourpalm again. Did you notice how it’s slightly firmer? A rare steak should feel like thiswhen you touch it.
3. Med-rare: Touch your thumb to your middle finger. Notice how that section of your palmtenses up a little more? This is what a medium-­rare steak feels like.
4. Medium: Move your thumb to your ring finger. Now the palm may feel slightly flexed.This level of firmness means your steak is cooked medium.
5. Well-done: Now touch your thumb to your pinky. That section of your palm will befairly firm. This is what a well-­done steak feels like.

Baking soda is sodium bicarbonate, which requires an acid and a liquid to become activated and help baked goods rise.
Baking powder includes sodium bicarbonate, as well as an acid. It only needs a liquid to become activated.

Gluten is a structural protein naturally found in wheat, rye, barley and triticale (across between rye and barley).
